## Summary

The `Person` aggregate stores direct identifiers in **plaintext** (SQLite): `emails`,
`phones`, `companies`, `socialProfiles` (JSON TEXT), plus name + address fields. GDPR
Art. 32 (security of processing) argues for field-level encryption at rest of contact
PII. This is a SEPARATE storage/crypto axis from the already-complete PII **egress**
redaction (`@/lib/pii`, ADR-032) — egress is done; at-rest is not.

## Design Questions (resolve FIRST — these ARE the entry gate)

1. **Which fields** need field-level encryption (names/emails/phones/address) vs stay
   plaintext for queryability (e.g. `addressCountryCode` must stay queryable for
   holiday/geo lookups)?
2. **Search/sort/filter impact:** encrypting a field breaks `WHERE`/`ORDER BY`/dedup on
   it. CRM list/search filters Persons today (`person.actions.ts` `{companies:{contains}}`
   etc.). Decide: blind-index (HMAC) for equality, client-side-only filtering, or a
   searchable normalized-hash column.
3. **Key management:** reuse `encryption.ts`; decryption server-only.
4. **Migration:** reversible, DRY_RUN-able backfill (plaintext → encrypted), per-row
   try/catch.
5. **Anonymize/merge interplay:** `AnonymizePerson` must still work on encrypted fields;
   `MergePersons` must decrypt→re-encrypt.
